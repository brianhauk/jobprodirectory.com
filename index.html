Find Better Jobs Faster

JobPro Directory saves you time finding and earning the attention of British Columbia employers you want to work for using easy to use employer research/ self-marketing tools combined with a user-updatable BC Employers database. Tutorials help guide you from being underemployed or unemployed to employed in 3 steps:


3 steps to a better job

1) Find and research employers to identify those you want to work for - by city, industry, company name, partial postal code, street or keywords. Mark them as Targeted Employers.

2) Find and research their decision makers and influencers to identify the person most likely to be your boss at each of these employers.

Learn their names, titles, email addresses, business needs, personal interests and other details you think can be useful in getting them to pay attention to you.

They should be potentially willing to recommend or hire you, or be replaced by more useful employer contacts. Add them as Targeted Contacts.

3) Sell yourself to your Targeted Contacts as a solution to their business problems using your knowledge of what's likely to impress them. Contact them through your network (or directly, if you know how to earn their attention) to maximize your chances of getting their attention & making a great first impression.


Target Decision Makers

Build a network of people willing to introduce or recommend you to decision makers (your Targeted Contacts) as a solution to their specific problems based on your special problem solving abilities.

Then, because you come recommended, and because these decision makers have a better understanding of your background, their decision to hire you or refer you to others is based on your ability to do the job, and not on your résumé keywords, answers to irrelevant interview questions or lack of specific experience.

This direct, proactive approach targeting employers and decision makers also enables you to avoid focusing your job search efforts on the public job market where HR departments and recruiters demand very specific experience and skills best learned on the job.

The result is you find better jobs faster while saving employers time and money hiring the right person.
