Welcome to this series of short JobPro Directory tutorial videos on finding the job you want (and can do) faster. My name is Brian Hauk, and I'm going to introduce you to JobPro Directory and an approach to using this job seeker resource to find and create job opportunities with local employers. Just like in a cooking show, we'll follow a recipe to create something special: the job you want and need to improve your life. Or, if you prefer following your own approach/recipes for finding good jobs, you can return to the main tutorial menu to view specific tutorials.

To follow the approach to finding work presented here, I think it's useful and motivating to understand that what makes this important goal obtainable is the fact that you need only to SUCCESSFULLY IDENTIFY AND TARGET ONE EMPLOYER AND ONE DECISION MAKER. ONE out of the thousands of employers and decision makers listed in JobPro Directory is all it takes to improve your life!

We will find them by industry category, geographic area like city and postal code, and by name. We will also see how to target several employers using the simple yet effective JOB SEARCH ACTION PLANS JobPro Directory creates for you to increase your ability to find good jobs faster.

We will use JobPro Directory to simplify researching employers and identifying the decision makers you need to connect with to get the job you want. We'll confirm and expand upon JobPro Directory's employer and employer decision maker information - after you decide you'd like to work with them to avoid wasting your time.

We will begin by using JobPro Directory employer listings and research tools to identify employers best matching your interests, work experience and career building requirements.

Then, as part of qualifying employers, we'll set JobPro Directory to show the commuting distance/time from your home to the employer's location so you can avoid targeting employers outside of what you consider to be a reasonable commuting distance (if you won't be working from home if hired).

We will also use your JobPro Directory-based employer research results to brand and market you as a solution to some of your targeted employers' problems.

And - based on your employer research results - we will try to anticipate what your targeted employers consider a "good fit" with their organizations and existing teams, knowing they are more likely to hire you if they perceive you as being eager to work with them and likely to energize their current employees.

In summary, these tutorials show you how to use JobPro Directory to:

  1. Find and research employers to identify those you want to work for - by city, industry, company name, partial postal code, street or keywords. Mark them as Targeted Employers.
  2. Find and research their decision makers & influencers to identify the people most likely to hire you or help you get hired. Add them as Targeted Contacts.
  3. Sell yourself to your Targeted Contacts using your knowledge of what's likely to make a great first impression with them. Then follow-up to maximize your chances of being hired.

NOT DISCUSSED is the importance of measuring your success and changing something if you're not achieving your job search milestones and goals. For example, you may need to improve your cover letter, résumé or employment proposal, follow-up "cold calling" script, or select better employer contacts if you are not getting interviews or invitations to meet while contacting your targeted decision makers.

Talk to your local job search assistance/ employment services provider or explore these ideas on how to impress employers when you need help. See all JobPro Directory tutorials.
